About Us

The Team

We are an FTC Robotics team from The Dalton School. The team was started in 2012 and has been competing ever since. We are a group of motivated students each with expertise in complimentary areas. We have dedicated many of our after school hours to building this robot to be a strong competitor in FTC's CenterStage game.

Motivate

Our software team has helped a fellow Dalton team, 11453 Uncertainty Principle, make their road runner work. In addition, we worked as consultant to the middle school play which was on robotics. We also have a well established meme page that inspires our 6,850+ followers through humor. Team members have also used our robotics knowledge to do research and lecture at NYU, in addition to working with the central park zoo, and mentoring at growing up green and multiple summer camps.

Connect

We’ve connected with the professional community in both learning new skills and bringing more STEM access to students citywide. We’ve met with Sean, a mechanical engineer, who guided us through setup steps to use a SLAM 3d mapping sensor, SLDWRKS, and fluid dynamics simulator. We’ve also been working with Mars Lee, a data analyst and comic artist, to bring free and open source software awareness in Python to high school students.

The Robot

Chasis

This season, we wanted a chassis that provided us with the greatest amount of internal space which would lend us the most flexibility when it came to prototyping and innovating designs. With this in mind, we knew we either needed to use a GoBILDA strafer chassis or an 80/20 customized chassis. Because of the amount of customizability that we needed, and the benefits of flexibility in motor heights and distances from the wheels, we decided to go with the 80/20 chassis.

Intake

In our intake mechanism, we decided to focus on reliability in the design. While picking up the pixels quickly is important, having them in a reliable orientation with a low probability of fallout was even more of a priority for this iteration. With this in mind, we designed a claw that uses herringbone gears to minimize slip, and contains TPU inserts to ensure adequate friction when grabbing onto pixels. Since 3d printing an intake does come with increased fragility, we ran simulations in Fusion360 on each of the components to model their behavior under stress.

Software

For our software, we implemented a modular structure for our mechanisms. In combination with roadrunner, this allowed us to organize our code for maximum legibility. In autonomous, we use a neural network custom-trained by our software team to detect our team prop.
